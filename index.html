2024 to Present
Journalism Instructor
Breakthrough Miami · Weekend Enrichment Program

I teach feature storytelling and human interest journalism to middle school students on weekends. My curriculum covers storytelling fundamentals, ethical reporting, interview technique, and on-camera presentation. I build lesson plans from scratch that translate professional newsroom standards into engaging, accessible instruction for students who have never stepped in front of a camera. Most of them have never considered journalism as a possibility. That changes after class.

About Me

I am a broadcast journalist, executive producer, and storyteller based in Miami, Florida. I am a junior at Gulliver Preparatory School, enrolled in the Digital Mass Media and Business and Entrepreneurship signature programs.

At RaiderVision, I serve as Executive Producer of Raider Rise and Chief of one of the feature news teams. I do not just appear on camera. I run the operation: editorial planning, production timelines, scripting, interviews, B-roll, and final edits, every week.

I have won three NATAS Student Production Awards, a distinction most professional journalists never achieve. I represented RaiderVision at the STN National Convention, where I received the Broadcast Excellence Award: Superior in Achievement in Scholastic Broadcast News.

Outside the studio, I teach feature storytelling and human interest journalism to middle school students through Breakthrough Miami. This summer, I am leading media and content creation at Camp Wayne for Girls.
